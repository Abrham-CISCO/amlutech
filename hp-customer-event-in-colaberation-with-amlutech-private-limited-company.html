     It is evident that advertising alone, no matter how sophisticated, is not sufficient to ensure that consumers are highly engaged with a brand. It is essential that companies review their Integrated Marketing Communication IMC activities as means of communicating with, rather than to, customers: listening to their concerns and discoursing on how best to address the latter. Although getting out to customers a  product’s message is important, the effectiveness of even the best advertising will be limited, if customers feel cutoff from the company, talked to but not listened to, or unable to interact with the company. Furthermore, customers not only want to learn about specific commitments as to what they can expect in dealing with the company, but, increasingly and surprisingly, are eager to find out what the company expects from them. Even the clearest of advertising promises needs to be coupled with two-way communications in which the company commits to listening to customer issues and responding to them.

 

     Hence, it is essential that they be made collaborative engagements, and not sales pitches, so that attendees feel more connected and involved. They should primarily serve as venues for demonstrating how clients’ successes can be achieved as well as occasions for giving all attendees something useful, regardless of whether they are doing business with the company or not. These will all build up and/or reinforce reputations, while potential customers will keep the company in mind whenever they need its products or services.

 

     Given the highly competitive nature of the market for ICT products and services in Ethiopia, as well as the multi-faceted beneficial nature of customer events, it is imperative that such proceedings be organized on a regular basis, preferably quarterly, but not less than twice a year.

 

The following discussion has been done with the target audience, timing, message content, and other aspects of the customer event.

 

Audience

     The customer event targeted Heads of Departments responsible for Supplies Management (Purchasing) as well as the Head and a staff member of the ICT of different Bureau at zonal administrative level in Oromia, Amhara and Southern Nations, Nationalities and Peoples Regional State and at the sub-city (kifle-ketema) administrative level in Addis Ababa City Administration, Different Financial Institute, Governmental Organization, Non Governmental Organization NGO and Higher Educational Institute.  The total number of invitees for the past 4 years are 2500. It is expected that 90% invitees will attend the meeting.

 

Message

      It is extremely important that the customer event be one at which the full range of HP’s products and services are brought to the attention of invitees, as this will enable them to have a better understanding of what is available, permit them to discover novel approaches for addressing their current needs, learn from lessons derived from success stories, and acquire an insight as to what is possible. Hence, it is important that presentations be ones that render a broad coverage of major products and services on offer, and not be limited to narrow topics, such as “Combating Counterfeits.”  Focus on what is new at HP, new products and services, and new directions are bound to be highly beneficial. Each presentation must be accompanied by pertinent documentation (brochures, information documents, etc.) for distribution to invitees.

 

Timing

Each quarter of the European calendar
